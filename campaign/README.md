## Strategy in one paragraph

Team Tally is free, offline, no-accounts — so there's no funnel to optimise and no
paywall to push. The whole job of this campaign is **awareness + a download**. We
lean on the one unfair advantage we already have: a hand-verified list of ~40
grassroots clubs with their Instagram and Facebook handles. Public posts build a bit
of ambient credibility ("this is a real thing, with a real website"); the direct
club DMs (already templated) do the actual converting. Posts and DMs reinforce each
other — a club that got a DM and then sees a launch post is far likelier to bite.

## Channels (ORB)

Everything points back to **teamtally.is** (owned) and the App Store / Play Store listing.

- **Owned** — `teamtally.is` landing page + waitlist, the **einargudni.com** blog post, the
  waitlist email (one "we're live" send on launch day).
- **Rented** — **Instagram** (primary; the club audience lives here), **Facebook**
  (primary for the many small clubs that are FB-only — see the outreach list),
  **LinkedIn** (secondary; founder/maker angle, English, drives the indie-hacker crowd
  to the blog).
- **Borrowed** — the **club outreach DMs** (`team-tally-outreach-templates.md`). This is
  the real engine. Launch posts give the DMs a credible backdrop. Later: ask the first
  1–2 clubs that adopt it for a quote / screenshot of their leaderboard to reshare.

## Phases (kept deliberately small)

1. **Tease** (Day -7 → -1): two posts that sell the *problem* (the messy Excel kitty,
   chasing people for cash) and one countdown. No product reveal yet beyond a teaser.
2. **Launch** (Day 0): announcement across IG + FB + LinkedIn, blog goes live,
   waitlist email goes out, and the **first wave of club DMs** starts the same day.
3. **Sustain** (Day +2 → +10): four feature-spotlight / engagement posts that each
   show one concrete thing the app does, ending on a soft "settle the kitty" CTA.

That's **8 social posts + 1 LinkedIn + 1 blog** over ~2.5 weeks. Minimal on purpose —
better to ship every planned post than to plan 30 and ghost after three.
