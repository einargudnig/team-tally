Most "team apps" want you to create an account, invite your teammates, and sync
everything to a server you'll forget you signed up for.

I built the opposite.

**Team Tally** launches today — a fine tracker for sports teams. Late to training,
forgot your kit, phone out in the huddle: tap, pick the player, pick the offence,
done. A live leaderboard shows who's topping the team table, and you settle the
kitty period by period.

The interesting constraint: **no backend, no accounts, no cloud.** Everything lives
in a local SQLite database on the captain's phone. Uninstall the app and the data is
gone — that's the entire privacy policy. It works offline because there's nothing to
be online *for*. For a locker-room tool used by one person on matchday, that turned
out to be exactly right: zero friction to start, nothing to administer, nothing to
breach.

It started with the most Icelandic of problems — every grassroots football team here
runs a fines kitty, and every one of them tracks it in a doomed shared spreadsheet.

Free on the App Store and Play Store. Built for the clubs in Iceland's lower
divisions first, but the kitty is universal.

Wrote up the why (and the case for boring, offline-first software) on my blog 👇
